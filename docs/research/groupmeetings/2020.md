## 2020-10-16 — Hao Xie

### Diffusion Monte Carlo method

#### Abstract:
Amongst the wide variety of quantum monte carlo methods, the DMC might be a quite different one. The basic idea is that one could extract the ground state by following evolution of the imaginary-time Schrodinger equation, in which the evolution operator $e^{-\tau H}$, also known as the propagator, or Green's function, acts as a projector onto the ground state when the imaginary time $\tau$ reaches infinity. By writing the propagator as a path integral, one could **sample** the wave function via a Markov-chain-like procedure through a combination of a diffusive displacement and a birth-and-death process.
However, the implication is that the ground-state wave function should be positive everywhere, which places severe restrictions on the DMC method. In particular, the many-body system of Fermions cannot be directly handled due to the presence of node surfaces.
As a result, the formulation of DMC has been improved to account for the notorious **Fermion sign problem**. The point is to impose certain node structure to the wave function in some way, possibly from the results obtained from other methods, e.g., variational monte carlo (VMC). This has become the way DMC is actually used in electron systems nowadays.
#### References
- Basics of DMC method: [Jos Thijssen, Computational Physics](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/computational-physics/BEE73B0139D4A9993193B57CDC62096E)
- Some recent progress regarding the Fermionic sign problem in QMC:
- [The free of sign problem by uncovering the mathematical structure of certain class of models](https://arxiv.org/abs/1506.05349).
- [Alleviation by means of automatic differentiation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2010.01141).

## 2020-09-04 — Hongbin Ren

### Scientific machine learning in quantum chemistry 1 -- Truly variational Hartree Fock

#### Abstract:
Hartree Fock method is a basic method in quantum chemistry, it usually comes with self consistent field (SCF) approach to find proper single particle wavefunction. Here, we do things differently, we are going to minimize the total energy of a quantum system against the one body density matrix as well as other related parameters. We show that the result of the new algorithm is competitive with original SCF based approach, and surpass the previous fully variational Hartree Fock paper.
