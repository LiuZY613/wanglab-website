### Error Estimations of Finite Temperature Lanczos Method

#### Abstract
Error Estimations of Finite Temperature Lanczos Method. For high-T region with T>1, err ~ 1/sqrt(R N) where R is the number of random sampling and N is the number of states involved, N-> Hilbert space dimension of Hamiltonian when T->infty, while for low temperature region, err ~1/sqrt(R)

## 2022-05-06 — Hao Xie

### Twist-averaged boundary conditions

#### Abstract
Twist-averaged boundary conditions is a useful method to alleviate the finite size effect in many-body computational approaches like QMC. The basic idea is illustrated via the exactly solvable case of free fermions. The Monkhorst-Pack construction of special k-points is commonly used to carry out the twist average, which has been implemented to calculate the quasiparticle effective mass of two-dimensional spin-polarized electron gas.
#### References
- [https://journals.aps.org/pre/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevE.64.016702](https://journals.aps.org/pre/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevE.64.016702)
- [https://journals.aps.org/prb/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevB.13.5188](https://journals.aps.org/prb/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevB.13.5188)
