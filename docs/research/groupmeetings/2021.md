## 2021-09-03 — Hao Xie

### jet: generalized "chain rule" for propagating higher-order derivatives

#### Abstract
Jet is a generalization of jvp in forward-mode automatic differentiation to computing higher-order derivatives. The basic idea is to propagate the differentials **of all orders** along the computation graph. Such an approach can avoid redundant efforts and be more efficient than conventional nested-jvp way as supported in present AD frameworks.

## 2021-04-02 — Hao Xie

### 1. A little subtle technical details about AD of dominant eigensolver of general non-symmetric matrices; 2. Solve rigorously the dimer covering problem using matrix Pfaffian

#### Abstract
1. Recently, Xingyu Zhang has been carrying out a vumps calculation involving AD of dominant eigen-decomposition of a general non-symmetric matrix, where only the right (say,) eigenvector is desired. The whole computation process is, nevertheless, gauge invariant. It is found that in such a case, the relevant backward formulas can be written in a way free of the corresponding left eigenvector, thus reducing the time for forward pass by a half.
2. Dimer covering on a graph is a classical problem rooted in statistical mechanics and chemistry, and has been extensively studied in the 1960s. It turns out the partition function can be written as a Pfaffian of the adjacency matrix of the graph, provided an appropriate orientation of the graph is chosen to make all the contribution terms have the same sign. For any planar graph, finding such an orientation is always possible and practical in   polynomial time.
Furthermore, the Ising model defined on arbitrary planar graphs (e.g., a square lattice) can be shown to map to the dimer covering problem on a different graph, with more vertices and edges added in a systematic way.
#### References
1. - [llustrative derivation by embedding the dominant eigensolver primitive in some auxiliary power iteration steps](https://code.itp.ac.cn/codes/groupmeeting/-/blob/master/resources/power.pdf)
- Original derivation using power method: [https://arxiv.org/abs/1906.09023](https://arxiv.org/abs/1906.09023)
2. - [http://www.phys.ens.fr/~jacobsen/AIMES/Dimers.pdf](http://www.phys.ens.fr/~jacobsen/AIMES/Dimers.pdf)
- For the relationship between dimer covering problem and Ising model (and more), say, e.g.: http://math.univ-lyon1.fr/homes-www/toninelli/noteDimeri_latest.pdf, [https://www.amazon.com/Two-Dimensional-Ising-Model-Second/dp/0486493350](https://www.amazon.com/Two-Dimensional-Ising-Model-Second/dp/0486493350)

## 2021-03-05 — Shuang Liang

### Kitaev model and Kitaev Materials

#### Abstract
The Kitaev spin liquid (KSL) has attracted great interest since its proposal by Kitaev over a decade ago as it provides a minimum and exactly solvable model to study quantum spin liquids(QSL). In this talk, we introduced the exact solution of the ground state, topological properties, and anyon excitations of the Kitaev model as well as the realization of Kitaev interaction in real materials. The topological phase transitions of KSL under magnetic field are also discussed, it suggested that there exists a novel intermediate topological phase between the low field Ising-topological phase and high field polarized phase. Finally, we talked about the experimental signatures of a QSL. It turns out the fractionalized spin excitations in QSL would generate unique features in the specific heat Cv-T and the NMR relaxation rate 1/T1-T curves, which would help us to identify a QSL state.
