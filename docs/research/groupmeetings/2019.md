## 2019-11-22 — Hao Xie

### Backward AD of dominant eigen-decomposition

#### Abstract
The backward automatic differentiation of the process of dominant eigen-decomposition of an arbitrary real, diagonalizable matrix is thoroughly studied.
The adjoint relation of the process can be derived by two different approaches, namely the so-called adjoint method and one based on full eigen-decomposition process. It turns out that in the general case, when the desired eigenvectors (as well as the eigenvalue) are needed for downstream computations, one can just solve one certain kind of low-rank linear system instead of fully diagonalize the matrix in order to obtain the full spectrum, which is very expensive when the matrix size is large. Both of the approaches yields completely the same results, however the one based on full eigen-decomposition reveals the mechanism of the avoiding direct access of the full spectrum information more clearly.
Furthermore, the low-rank linear system appearing in the backward pass of dominant eigen-decomposition can be differentiated again, yield another low-rank linear system of the same kind. This makes it possible to compute arbitrarily high order derivatives of the dominant eigen-decomposition process.
#### Applications
- The various order derivative of the ground state energy and fidelity susceptibility of the 1D Transverse Field Ising Model.
- Gradient-based optimization of uniform MPS, which can be used for variational calculation of the ground state energy of various 1D lattice models, such as TFIM.

- [https://buwantaiji.github.io/2019/10/CG-backward/](https://buwantaiji.github.io/2019/10/CG-backward/)
